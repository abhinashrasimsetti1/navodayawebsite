Our History

Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas (JNVs) were established in 1986 by the Government of India as a system of residential schools under the National Policy on Education. Named after Jawaharlal Nehru, these schools aim to provide quality education to gifted children, primarily from rural areas, through the JNVST entrance exam. Today, there are over 650 JNVs across India, each a beacon of educational excellence and social inclusivity.

Vision & Mission

To provide equal opportunities to talented children from all parts of India, especially rural areas, to access quality residential education that nurtures their academic, physical, and moral development, enabling them to become leaders and responsible citizens of tomorrow.
